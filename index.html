HeyGen Video Dubbing Benchmark

Evaluating AI-powered video dubbing across 47 clips and 40 languages

March 2026 — LTX Research

Key Takeaways

  • 93% overall success rate across 1,880 dubbing jobs (47 clips × 40 languages)
  • Frontal dialogue works well — good lip sync, voice quality, and accent preservation for standard talking-head content
  • Non-human and animated characters handled surprisingly well — CGI, animated, and masked faces produce quality results
  • Singing is a non-starter — the model speaks lyrics instead of singing them, losing rhythm and melody entirely
  • Distinctive voices and strong emotions are lost — unique voices become generic, screaming becomes calm, crying becomes flat

Overview

We benchmarked HeyGen's video dubbing (via LTX Studio) on a curated set of 47 video clips spanning 17 categories: frontal dialogue, side profiles, occlusion, singing, animation, CGI, action, and more. Each clip was dubbed into 40 languages covering European, Asian, Middle Eastern, and African language families.

Methodology

Each video was processed through the LTX Studio API, which wraps HeyGen's /v2/video_translate endpoint. Lip sync was enabled for all runs. The pipeline:

Upload video → LTX Studio API → HeyGen dubbing → Download result

All 40 languages were batched into a single task per video. Typical processing time: 8–10 minutes per clip (all languages in parallel). Results were manually reviewed for lip sync quality, voice consistency, background preservation, and translation fidelity.

What Needs Improvement

Singing

The model cannot make characters sing. It speaks the lyrics instead, completely losing rhythm and melody. Background music is preserved but the vocal performance is lost.

A Star Is Born — Lady Gaga singing 'Shallow'

Translated (Greek)

Side Profile Lip Sync

Side-profile shots produce blurry, jittering lips. Especially problematic when combined with eating or hand-to-face gestures.

Temporary Lip Occlusion

When an object briefly passes over the lips (hand, person, microphone), the generated lips jitter and produce visual artifacts. Unlike full occlusion which works well, intermittent occlusion is problematic.

Lips Generated on Wrong Surface

Emotional Intensity

Strong emotion in the original is lost in translation. The king screams 'forget about the blessed shilling' with real fury, but the dubbed voice sounds calm and measured. The model flattens vocal intensity regardless of the original performance.

Non-Standard Speech Patterns

Stammering, gurgling, and non-speech vocalizations are removed. The model normalizes all speech, losing important character and narrative details.

Non-Verbal Vocalizations Removed

Non-speech vocalizations that are integral to the scene — such as gurgling, choking, or struggling to speak — are completely stripped out. The model only translates clean words, losing vocalizations that carry narrative meaning.

Overlapping Speakers

When two speakers talk simultaneously or one cuts into another, the model mutes one of them entirely. The anchor on the left speaks during the clip but all translations silence her, producing a monologue instead of the original crosstalk.

In some clips the dubbed speech drifts noticeably out of sync with the mouth movements. The cause is unclear — it could be the heavy shadows on the face, the slight camera angle, the background music, or a combination. The audio itself is well-preserved.
